## Recap

- **Unit 023 — is done / was done (Passive 1):** present simple passive (*is/are + past participle*) for policies and general truths, past simple passive (*was/were + past participle*) for completed past events — practiced through a post-incident Slack thread ("The root cause **was identified**", "All P1s **are reviewed**").
- **Unit 024 — is being done / has been done (Passive 2):** present continuous passive (*is/are being + past participle*) for actions happening right now, present perfect passive (*has/have been + past participle*) for recently completed actions whose result matters now — practiced through a live incident status thread ("The node **is being replaced**", "The customers **have been notified**").
- **Unit 025 — have something done:** the causative structure *have/get + object + past participle*, for arranging someone else to do something for you (or something happening to you) — practiced through a 1:1 about a database migration ("We **had it reviewed** by the DBA team").
- **Unit 026 — Reported speech (He said that...):** backshift — present → past, *will → would*, *can → could*, present perfect → past perfect — practiced through Levi relaying a tech lead's outage update ("She said the root cause **was** a bad config push").
- **Unit 027 — Reported speech (2): questions and requests:** yes/no questions reported with *if/whether*, wh-questions keep the question word but lose inversion, orders/requests use *tell/ask + object + to-infinitive* — practiced through Levi briefing a peer after a 1:1 ("She asked **if** I **was happy**...", "She **told me to** send her the doc").
- **Unit 028 — Used to (do):** past habits/states that are no longer true, no present form, negative/question drop the *-d* (*didn't use to*, *did...use to*) — practiced through Levi catching up with an old colleague ("I **used to write** code all day, but now...").

## 8 Mixed Exercises

Transform or complete each item using the structure indicated. Answers are at the end — don't peek early.

1. **(Unit 23 — passive present simple)** Transform: *"The security team audits all deployments."* → *"All deployments ___ ___ by the security team."*
2. **(Unit 24 — present continuous passive)** Transform: *"The team is refactoring the payment service right now."* → *"The payment service ___ ___ ___ right now."*
3. **(Unit 24 — present perfect passive)** Transform: *"Someone has approved the PR."* → *"The PR ___ ___ ___."*
4. **(Unit 25 — causative)** Complete: *"We ___ the CI pipeline ___ (migrate) by the platform team last sprint."*
5. **(Unit 26 — reported statement)** Direct: *"I will send the postmortem by Friday."* → *"She said ___ ___ ___ the postmortem by Friday."*
6. **(Unit 27 — reported yes/no question)** Direct: *"Has the rollback been tested?"* → *"He asked ___ the rollback ___ ___ ___."*
7. **(Unit 27 — reported wh-question)** Direct: *"Where is the latency spike coming from?"* → *"She asked ___ the latency spike ___ ___ ___."*
8. **(Unit 28 — used to, negative)** Complete: *"The team ___ ___ ___ ___ (not/use to) have an on-call rotation."*

### ✅ Answers

1. *"All deployments **are audited** by the security team."*
2. *"The payment service **is being refactored** right now."*
3. *"The PR **has been approved**."*
4. *"We **had** the CI pipeline **migrated** by the platform team last sprint."*
5. *"She said **she would send** the postmortem by Friday."*
6. *"He asked **if/whether** the rollback **had been tested**."*
7. *"She asked **where** the latency spike **was coming from**."*
8. *"The team **didn't use to** have an on-call rotation."*

## Integrative Writing Task (self-directed)

Write a short paragraph (5–7 sentences) about a recent project at work, using at least **3 of the 6 structures** from this week (passive present/past, passive continuous/perfect, causative *have/get something done*, reported speech, reported questions/requests, *used to*).

**Model paragraph:**

> Our observability stack **used to** rely on three disconnected tools, so last quarter we **had** the entire pipeline **redesigned** by a senior SRE we brought in as a consultant. The new dashboards **are being rolled out** to every team this month, and the migration script **has already been tested** in staging. When I briefed our VP, she **asked whether** the rollout **would affect** the on-call rotation, and I told her it wouldn't — alerts **are still routed** the same way. She **said** she **was glad** the change **had been vetted** so thoroughly before going company-wide.

*(Structures used: used to, causative, present continuous passive, present perfect passive, reported question, present simple passive, reported statement.)*

## 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

Esta semana consolidamos seis estruturas que se conectam entre si. As quatro formas de **voz passiva** (*is done, was done, is being done, has been done*) já devem estar bem sólidas — foram praticadas em dois dias seguidos com o mesmo contexto (incidentes e status de produção), então a distinção entre "política geral" (*is/are*), "evento passado" (*was/were*), "acontecendo agora" (*is/are being*) e "concluído recentemente" (*has/have been*) tende a estar mais automática. A estrutura causativa (*have/get something done*) e o discurso indireto (*reported speech*, incluindo perguntas e pedidos) provavelmente ainda pedem atenção — são construções com mais peças móveis (ordem de palavras, escolha entre *if/whether* e wh-words, backshift de tempo verbal) e costumam exigir mais um ou dois exemplos para "grudar". *Used to* tende a ser a mais intuitiva das seis, mas vale reforçar a armadilha ortográfica (*used to* vs. *use to* após *did/didn't*).
