### 2. 🧠 Mini-context

> **CEO:** **Are** we **still rolling out** the copilot to all three units?
> **You:** Yes — two are live, the third goes next week.
> **CEO:** And **why is** adoption **slowing** in Finance?
> **You:** They're short-staffed this month. **What are** you **hoping** to see
> by the review?
> **CEO:** Honestly? **Are** the numbers **moving** yet, or not.

Notice the shape before any rule: to ask, the *to be* verb (**are / is**) jumps
**in front of** the subject, and the **-ing** verb stays put — *Are we rolling…?
Why is adoption slowing? What are you hoping…?*

---

### 3. 📐 A regra (form)

Statement → question: move **am / is / are** to the front of the subject.

| | to be | subject | verb-ing | rest |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Yes/No | **Are** | you | presenting | today? |
| Yes/No | **Is** | the board | reviewing | the budget? |
| Wh- | **Why is** | adoption | slowing | in Finance? |
| Wh- | **What are** | we | investing in | this year? |

**Structure:** *(question word) + am/is/are + subject + verb-ing?*

- **Yes/No question:** *to be* comes first → *"**Are** you **hiring**?"*
- **Wh- question:** the question word comes first, then *to be*, then subject →
  *"**Where are** you **presenting**?"*
- **Short answers** use only *to be*, not the -ing verb: *"**Are** you joining?"
  → "Yes, I **am**." / "No, I**'m not**."*

---

### 4. 🎯 Quando usar — e quando não (THE core section)

**Why does this structure exist?** It's the question form of yesterday's
"live-camera" tense. You use it to ask about something **in progress right now
or around now** — *Are the numbers moving? Is the team still migrating?* — not
about habits or permanent facts.

**Decision rule — continuous question vs. present-simple question (the rival
that matters most).** Same as Unit 003, but now in question form:

| | Present continuous question | Present simple question |
|---|---|---|
| Asks about | something happening **now / temporarily** | a **habit, routine, permanent fact** |
| Form | *am/is/are* + subject + **-ing** | ***do/does*** + subject + **base verb** |
| Example | *Are you **presenting** the vision today?* | *Do you **present** to the board often?* |
| Test | "right now / these days" | "usually / in general" |

Minimal pair — same words, different question:
- *Are you **leading** the AI task force?* → is this your job **right now**,
  this quarter?
- *Do you **lead** the AI task force?* → is this your **general role**?

**The word-order trap.** This is the #1 error. English asks by **inverting** —
*to be* before the subject. Portuguese asks mostly with **intonation**, keeping
statement order (*"Você está apresentando hoje?"* = statement word order + a
rising tone). Translated word-for-word you get *"You are presenting today?"* —
understandable, but not how English forms a real question. English **must**
flip: *"**Are you** presenting today?"*

**PT→EN mapping.** *"Está + gerúndio?"* maps to *"Is/Are … + -ing?"* — the
meaning transfers well. Two places it betrays Levi:
- **Word order** (above): don't keep the subject first. Flip *to be* forward.
- **Wh- questions:** in a *why/what/where* question the *to be* still goes
  **before** the subject — *"**Why is** the board hesitating?"*, never *"Why the
  board is hesitating?"*

**Top Brazilian-speaker errors:**
- ❌ *You are joining the strategy call?* → ✅ ***Are** you joining the strategy
  call?* — *a question inverts* to be *to the front; statement order + a
  question mark isn't enough.*
- ❌ *Why the CFO is pushing back?* → ✅ *Why **is** the CFO pushing back?* —
  *after the wh-word, the verb still comes before the subject.*
- ❌ *Do you presenting today?* → ✅ ***Are** you presenting today?* — *the
  continuous uses* are/is/am*, not* do*; mixing the two rivals breaks the form.*

### 5. ✏️ 8 annotated examples

1. **Are** we **still rolling out** the copilot to all three units? — *asks about
   an initiative in progress now → continuous question, not "Do we roll out".*
2. **Why is** adoption **slowing** in Finance this month? — *wh- question:* is
   *stays before the subject; "this month" frames a temporary trend.*
3. **What are** you **hoping** to see by the board review? — *a live expectation
   around now; simple "What do you hope" would sound like a general habit.*
4. **Is** the data team **still migrating** the pipeline, or are they done? —
   *asks whether an action is ongoing right now → continuous.*
5. **Are** the numbers **moving** yet? — *short, high-stakes: is the change
   happening at this moment? Continuous captures "in motion now".*
6. **Where are** you **presenting** the 2027 vision — the boardroom or remote? —
   *a scheduled action around now; "Where do you present" would ask about a
   routine.*
7. **Are** you **taking** on the innovation portfolio this quarter, or just
   advising? — *temporary assignment in progress → continuous, not "Do you
   take".*
8. **Are** the kids **sleeping** yet? — *everyday life; asks about a state
   happening right now.*

### 8. 🗣️ Para levar para a aula

- Ask your tutor to play the CEO firing **rapid status questions** at you —
  *"Are we rolling out on time? Why is Finance behind? What are you watching this
  week?"* — and answer each in one breath. The goal is to **hear the inversion**
  land automatically: *to be* forward, subject second, -ing verb last.
- Then a **flip-drill**: your tutor gives a continuous **statement** (*"The team
  is rebuilding the pipeline"*) and you instantly turn it into both a **yes/no**
  question (*"Is the team rebuilding the pipeline?"*) and a **wh-** question
  (*"Why is the team rebuilding the pipeline?"*), so the word order stops being a
  decision and becomes reflex.

### 10. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

**A regra e quando usar.** Para perguntar no *present continuous*, o verbo *to
be* (**am/is/are**) vai **para a frente do sujeito**, e o verbo em **-ing** fica:
*"**Are** you **presenting** today?"*, *"**Why is** adoption **slowing**?"*. Serve
para perguntar sobre algo **acontecendo agora ou temporariamente**. Contraste com
a pergunta no *present simple* (**do/does** + verbo base), que pergunta sobre
**hábito ou rotina**: *"**Do** you **present** to the board often?"*. Decida pela
duração: agora/estes dias → continuous; sempre/em geral → simple.

**As armadilhas.** (1) A maior é a **ordem das palavras**: o português pergunta
pela entonação mantendo a ordem da afirmação (*"Você está apresentando hoje?"*),
mas o inglês **obriga a inversão** — *"**Are you** presenting today?"*, nunca
*"You are presenting today?"*. (2) Em perguntas com *why/what/where*, o *to be*
continua **antes** do sujeito: *"Why **is** the board hesitating?"*, jamais *"Why
the board is hesitating?"*. (3) Não misture os rivais: continuous usa *are/is/am*,
não *do* — *"**Are** you joining?"*, não *"Do you joining?"*.
