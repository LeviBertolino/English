## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Levi:** What were you working on when the incident started?
> **Ana:** I was reviewing the deployment scripts — we were migrating the database schema at that exact moment.
> **Levi:** So the service was running, but the migration wasn't finishing?
> **Ana:** Right. The team wasn't watching the metrics dashboard, so nobody noticed the lag was building up.
> **Levi:** Were all three services affected, or just the primary?
> **Ana:** Just the primary. The secondaries were processing requests fine — they weren't hitting the same bottleneck.

Notice the pattern: *were you working*, *I was reviewing*, *we were migrating*, *the service was running*, *wasn't finishing*, *wasn't watching*, *was building up*, *Were all three affected*, *were processing*, *weren't hitting*. These are all **past continuous** — actions or situations that were **in progress** at some point in the past.

### The rule

**Past continuous** = **was / were + verb-ing**

**Positive:**

| Subject | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I / he / she / it | **was** + -ing | *The pipeline was running.* |
| you / we / they | **were** + -ing | *We were deploying when it failed.* |

**Negative:** Subject + **wasn't / weren't** + -ing
> *The alerts weren't firing. / I wasn't monitoring the queue.*

**Question:** **Was / Were** + subject + -ing?
> *Was the rollback script running? / Were you in the incident call?*

**When to use it — two main situations:**

**1. Action in progress at a specific past time:**
> *At 11pm, I was debugging the memory leak.* (= in progress at 11pm)
> *This time last week, we were preparing the quarterly roadmap.*

**2. Background / interrupted action** — something was in progress *when* a shorter event occurred:
> *I was reviewing the PR **when** my phone went off.* (was reviewing = in progress → phone went off = interruption, past simple)
> *We were running load tests **when** the database crashed.*

The **past simple** names the interruption; the **past continuous** sets the scene.

## 3. ✏️ 8 Example Sentences

1. **The CI/CD pipeline was processing 400 builds when the storage quota ran out** — everything queued up behind it.
2. **Were you attending the architecture review when the P0 alert fired?** I wasn't — I was on a call with a customer.
3. **Our AI team was fine-tuning the embedding model all week**; they weren't testing in production yet.
4. **I was writing the RFC when I realised the approach conflicted** with the new compliance requirements.
5. **The load balancer was routing traffic normally**, but two of the health-check endpoints weren't responding.
6. **She wasn't sleeping well last month** — the on-call rotation was hitting her really hard.
7. **Were you waiting long?** I was stuck in a retro that ran 20 minutes over.
8. **It was raining when I left the office** — not the best night for a production deploy.

---

## 4. ⚠️ 3 Common Mistakes

❌ **"I was work on the incident"** → ✅ **"I was working on the incident"**
*Why: Past continuous always needs -ing. "was work" leaves out the key -ing marker that tells you it's continuous.*

❌ **"I were working late when the alert came in"** → ✅ **"I was working late when the alert came in"**
*Why: "I" always takes "was", not "were". Only you/we/they use "were".*

❌ **"We were deploying when the server crashed, so we were calling the on-call engineer"** → ✅ **"We were deploying when the server crashed, so we called the on-call engineer"**
*Why: The interrupting or following action uses past simple, not past continuous. Past continuous = the ongoing backdrop; past simple = what happened next.*

## 6. 💡 Memory Tip

Think of past continuous as **a paused video frame**: the action was already running before the moment you're describing, and it kept going after. Past simple is **a snapshot** — it happened and finished.

> 🎬 *was/were + -ing* = video mid-play (in progress)
> 📸 *verb-ed / irregular past* = still photo (completed)

Classic pattern: *"I was [video] when [snapshot]."*
> *I was deploying (video) when the tests failed (snapshot).*

## 7. 🎤 Pronunciation Guide

### Key words and IPA

| Word / Phrase | IPA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| was (weak) | /wəz/ | Almost "wuz" — most common in connected speech |
| was (strong) | /wɒz/ | Full vowel — used only when stressed: *"Yes, I WAS"* |
| were (weak) | /wə/ | Nearly "wuh" — very reduced ⚠️ |
| were (strong) | /wɜː/ | Used when stressed: *"Yes, we WERE"* |
| wasn't | /ˈwɒzənt/ | Two syllables: *WOZ-nt*; no extra vowel after the t ⚠️ |
| weren't | /ˈwɜːənt/ | *WER-nt*; the cluster is tricky ⚠️ |
| working | /ˈwɜːkɪŋ/ | Ends in /ɪŋ/ — no extra vowel after ⚠️ |
| was working | /wəz ˈwɜːkɪŋ/ | "wuz WER-king" in natural speech |
| were you | /wə jʊ/ | Fast speech: nearly "wuh-yuh" |

### Tips for Brazilian Portuguese speakers

- **Weak "was" → /wəz/**: In connected speech, "was" is almost never the full /wɒz/. It sounds like a quick *"wuz"* with a schwa. Brazilians sometimes stress it too much. Keep it light. ⚠️
- **Weak "were" → /wə/**: Even more reduced — just a quick *"wuh"*. *"We were working"* sounds like *"wee-wuh-working"* in natural speech. The strong form /wɜː/ is reserved for stress/emphasis. ⚠️
- **The -ing ending /ɪŋ/**: Brazilians often append a vowel: *"working-i"* or *"deploy-ing-gee"*. The /ŋ/ is a nasal stop — the sound ends at the back of your throat with no following vowel. Practice closing your mouth at the /ŋ/. ⚠️
- **"wasn't" final cluster /zənt/**: The /t/ at the end is often a glottal stop in British English. Don't add a final "i" sound — it's *WOZ-nt*, not *WOZ-en-tchi*. ⚠️
- **Sentence rhythm**: In *"I was reviewing the PR when..."*, the stress falls on the content verb — *re-VIEW-ing* — not on "was". "Was" is almost background noise.

## 10. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

O **past continuous** (passado contínuo) expressa uma ação que estava *em andamento* em determinado momento do passado. A forma é **was/were + verbo-ing**: *"I"*, *"he/she/it"* usam *was*; *"you/we/they"* usam *were*. Negativo: *wasn't/weren't + -ing*; pergunta: *Was/Were + sujeito + -ing?* Dois usos principais: (1) ação em progresso numa hora específica — *"At 2am, I was debugging"*; (2) ação de fundo interrompida por outro evento — *"I was reviewing the PR when the alert fired"* — aqui o past continuous cria o cenário e o past simple narra a interrupção.

Na pronúncia, atenção a dois pontos críticos: *"was"* e *"were"* em fala conectada são extremamente reduzidos — *"was"* vira /wəz/ ("wuz") e *"were"* vira /wə/ ("wuh"), quase imperceptíveis. E o sufixo *-ing* termina em /ɪŋ/ — um nasal fechado, sem vogal depois. *"Working"* é /ˈwɜːkɪŋ/, não "working-i". Esses dois hábitos (reduzir o auxiliar e fechar o -ing) vão fazer sua fala soar muito mais natural.
