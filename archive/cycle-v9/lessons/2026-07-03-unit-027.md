# Unit 027 — Reported speech (2): questions and requests

## 0. 🔁 Warm-up: Quick Recall (≈60 seconds)

Yesterday's lesson — **Unit 026: Reported speech (He said that...)** — covered **backshift**: when you report what someone said, the verb usually moves one tense back (*"I **need** more time"* → *she said she **needed** more time*; *"I **will send** it"* → *she said she **would send** it*). You practiced this with Levi reporting a tech lead's update on an outage: *"She said the root cause **was** a bad config push."*

Three rapid recall questions:

1. Backshift this: *"I **am testing** it."* → He said he ___.
2. What's wrong with *"She told that the deploy failed"*?
3. When can you skip backshift entirely?

👉 **Answers:**
1. ✅ *"He said he **was testing** it."*
2. ✅ *tell* needs a person — *"She told **me** (that) the deploy failed"* or *"She **said** (that) the deploy failed."*
3. ✅ When the reported fact is still true or a general truth (*"She said the sun rises in the east"*)

## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Levi, briefing a peer after a 1:1 with his manager:**
>
> **Peer:** How did the 1:1 go?
>
> **Levi:** Good. She **asked me if** I **was happy** with the current team structure, and **asked how** the migration **was going**.
>
> **Peer:** Did she ask about the promo case?
>
> **Levi:** Yeah, she **asked whether** I **had submitted** the doc yet, and **asked when** I **would have** it ready.
>
> **Peer:** Any action items?
>
> **Levi:** She **told me to** send her the doc by Thursday, and **asked me not to** wait until the last minute this time.

Notice:
- Reported **yes/no questions** use **if** or **whether**: *"Are you happy?"* → *she asked if I was happy*.
- Reported **wh-questions** keep the question word but drop the question word order: *"How is the migration going?"* → *she asked how the migration was going* (NOT *how was the migration going*).
- No question mark, no auxiliary inversion (**do/does/did** disappear), and the verb still backshifts.
- Reported **orders/requests** use **tell/ask + object + to-infinitive**: *"Send me the doc"* → *she told me to send the doc*. Negative: *"Don't wait"* → *she asked me not to wait*.

### The rule

| Direct question | Reported question |
|---|---|
| *"**Are** you happy?"* (yes/no) | She asked **if/whether** I **was** happy. |
| *"**Do** you like it?"* | He asked **if** I **liked** it. |
| *"**Where** do you live?"* (wh-) | She asked **where** I **lived**. |
| *"**What time** does it start?"* | He asked **what time** it **started**. |

| Direct order/request | Reported order/request |
|---|---|
| *"**Close** the ticket."* | She **told me to close** the ticket. |
| *"Please **review** this."* | He **asked me to review** it. |
| *"**Don't deploy** on Friday."* | She **told me not to deploy** on Friday. |

> ⚠️ Statement word order in reported questions: **subject before verb**, no *do/does/did*. ❌ *"She asked where **did I go**"* → ✅ *"She asked where **I went**."*
> ⚠️ **say/ask/tell**: use *ask* for questions, *tell* for statements/orders directed at someone: *she told me to fix it* (not *said me*).

## 3. ✏️ 8 example sentences

1. The tech lead **asked if** the rollback **had been tested** in staging.
2. My manager **asked whether** I **was available** for the client call on Monday.
3. She **asked where** the latency spike **was coming from**.
4. The PM **asked when** the feature **would be** ready for release.
5. He **told** the on-call engineer **to escalate** immediately.
6. The interviewer **asked how long** I **had been working** with distributed systems.
7. My friend **asked if** I **wanted** to grab lunch later.
8. She **asked me not to share** the roadmap before the announcement.

## 4. 🛠️ Practice — recognition + production

**Recognition/gap-fill:**
Convert to reported speech: Direct: *"Did you fix the bug?"* → Reported: *"She asked ___ I ___ the bug."*

✅ **Answer:** *"She asked **if/whether** I **had fixed** the bug."*

**Production task:**
Write 2 sentences reporting a question or request your manager or a teammate recently asked you at work, using reported speech.

**Model answer:**
- *"He asked whether the API changes were backward-compatible."*
- *"She told me to update the runbook before the next on-call rotation."*

---

## 5. 💡 Memory tip

**Questions lose their question shape.** Once a question goes "into reported speech," it stops acting like a question: no inversion, no *do/does/did*, no question mark — it becomes a plain statement introduced by *if/whether* or a question word. Think of it as the question putting on a statement's clothes.

## 8. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

Hoje continuamos o discurso indireto, agora com **perguntas e pedidos**. Para perguntas de sim/não, usamos **if** ou **whether**: *"Are you happy?"* vira *"She asked if I was happy."* Para perguntas com wh- (*where, when, how*...), mantemos a palavra interrogativa mas voltamos à ordem de afirmação (sujeito antes do verbo), sem *do/does/did* e sem ponto de interrogação: *"Where do you live?"* vira *"She asked where I lived"* — nunca *"where did I live"*.

Para ordens e pedidos, usamos **tell/ask + pessoa + to + infinitivo**: *"Close the ticket"* vira *"She told me to close the ticket"*, e na negativa, *"Don't deploy on Friday"* vira *"She told me not to deploy on Friday."* Cuidado para não confundir *ask* (perguntar) com *tell* (dizer/mandar) — e nunca esquecer o pronome depois de *tell*.
