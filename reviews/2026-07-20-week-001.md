# 🔁 Review Week 001 — Units 1 to 7

Seven days, one big theme: the **present tense**. You built the *to be* verb
(am/is/are) and the two present tenses (simple and continuous), in all three
shapes — **statement, negative, question**. This week is not new grammar; it's
about making the *choices between* these forms automatic, because in real speech
they collide constantly. Read time: ~15 minutes.

---

## 1. 📋 Recap — one line per unit

- **Unit 001 — am / is / are (statements).** The linking verb: identity, state,
  location. Form follows the subject (*I am, she is, they are*). English never
  drops it; it covers both *ser* and *estar*.
- **Unit 002 — am / is / are (questions).** Ask by **inverting**: the verb jumps
  in front of the subject — *"**Are** we ready?"*. Never add *do* to *to be*.
- **Unit 003 — present continuous (I am doing).** *am/is/are* + **-ing** for
  action **in progress now / temporary**. The "live camera" tense.
- **Unit 004 — present continuous questions (Are you doing?).** Invert *to be*,
  keep the -ing — *"**Why is** adoption **slowing**?"*.
- **Unit 005 — present simple (I do / work / like).** Base verb for **habits,
  routines, general truths**; **-s** on he/she/it (*she leads*).
- **Unit 006 — present simple negative (I don't).** *don't / doesn't* + **base
  verb**; the -s migrates onto the auxiliary (*she **doesn't** lead*).
- **Unit 007 — present simple questions (Do you...?).** *do / does* + subject +
  **base verb**; the -s lands on *does* (*"**Does** she **lead**?"*).

## 2. 🗺️ Confusion map — when each form wins

The whole week turns on **three decisions**. Get these three reflexes and every
sentence this week falls into place.

### Decision A — *to be* or an action verb? (which "engine" runs the sentence)

| The sentence is about… | Engine | Statement | Negative | Question |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| what something **is / is like / where** | ***to be*** (am/is/are) | *She **is** ready.* | *She **isn't** ready.* | ***Is** she ready?* |
| an **action** (lead, fund, approve) | **do-support** | *She **leads**.* | *She **doesn't** lead.* | ***Does** she lead?* |

The trap: mixing engines. *to be* **inverts** and never borrows *do*
(❌ *Do you be ready?*). Action verbs **borrow do/does** and never invert
(❌ *Approve you this?*).

### Decision B — happening **now**, or **in general**? (simple vs continuous)

| | Present **continuous** | Present **simple** |
|---|---|---|
| Meaning | in progress **now / temporary** | **habit, routine, permanent fact** |
| Form | *am/is/are* + **-ing** | base verb (**+s** for he/she/it) |
| Signal words | *right now, at the moment, this week* | *usually, every, in general, how often* |
| Example | *We **are hiring** two leads this quarter.* | *We **hire** through referrals.* |

Decide by **duration**, never by translating the Portuguese tense directly —
Brazilian Portuguese often uses the simple present for *right now*
(*"eu trabalho nisso agora"* → *"I **am working** on that now"*).

### Decision C — where does the **-s** live?

The third-person -s exists **once** per verb phrase, and it moves:

- Statement → on the **main verb**: *The CFO **reports**.*
- Negative → on the **auxiliary**: *The CFO **doesn't report**.* (never *doesn't reports*)
- Question → on the **auxiliary**: ***Does** the CFO **report**?* (never *Does … reports*)

Never mark it twice, and never drop it entirely.

## 3. 🧪 10 recognition exercises (contrast-focused)

Retrieve first — all answers with explanations are in section 4.

1. **Simple or continuous?** *"We ___ a portfolio review every quarter."* —
   **run** or **are running**?
2. **Simple or continuous?** *"Right now the market ___ toward AI-first
   players."* — **shifts** or **is shifting**?
3. **Which engine (question)?** *"___ you confident about the plan?"* — **Are**
   or **Do**?
4. **Which engine (question)?** *"___ you approve budgets at this level?"* —
   **Are** or **Do**?
5. **Fix if needed:** *"Why the board is delaying the AI budget?"*
6. **Fix if needed:** *"He doesn't approves early-stage bets."*
7. **Fix if needed:** *"The market very competitive right now."*
8. **Simple or continuous (question)?** *"___ the CFO ___ the AI spend every
   quarter?"* — **Does / review** or **Is / reviewing**?
9. **Fix the negative:** *"We not invest in unproven vendors."*
10. **Which engine + tense?** *"___ we ___ out the copilot to all three units
    right now?"* — **Are / rolling** or **Do / roll**?

## 4. 🗣️ Para levar para a aula

Bring the **most confusable contrasts**, not the individual forms:

- **The now-vs-general switch (Decision B).** Ask your tutor to name one activity
  — *hiring, reviewing, presenting* — and fire it at you two ways back-to-back:
  *"What do you do?"* (role) vs *"What are you doing?"* (this moment). You answer
  each with the matching tense until the choice stops being a decision. This is
  the single highest-value drill of the week.
- **The two question engines (Decision A).** Have your tutor mix *to be* prompts
  and action-verb prompts at random — *"you / confident"*, *"the board / approve
  this"*, *"the team / still migrating"* — and you build the right question every
  time: *"Are you confident?"* vs *"Does the board approve this?"* vs *"Is the
  team still migrating?"*. Goal: stop reaching for *do* where *to be* should flip,
  and vice versa.
- **The travelling -s (Decision C).** In a 60-second description of your
  operating model, have your tutor stop you on any dropped, doubled, or misplaced
  -s: *she **reports*** / *she **doesn't report*** / *does she **report***.

## 5. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

**O que já deve estar sólido.** Toda a semana é o **presente**. Você tem agora as
três formas do *to be* (afirmação, negativa, pergunta) e os dois tempos do
presente — *simple* (hábitos, rotinas, verdades gerais) e *continuous*
(acontecendo agora, temporário). Três decisões resolvem tudo: **(A)** o
"motor" da frase — *to be* **inverte** e nunca usa *do*, verbos de ação usam
**do/does** e nunca invertem; **(B)** agora ou em geral — *is doing* para o que
está em andamento, *does* para hábito; **(C)** o **-s** da terceira pessoa vive
**uma vez só** e "migra" para o auxiliar na negativa e na pergunta
(*she reports → she doesn't report → does she report?*).

**O que provavelmente ainda precisa de trabalho.** Duas armadilhas de
interferência do português costumam sobreviver: **(1)** perguntar só pela
entonação, mantendo a ordem da afirmação — em inglês é obrigatório inverter o
*to be* (*"Are you ready?"*) ou usar *do/does* (*"Do you approve?"*), nunca
*"You are ready?"* / *"You approve?"*; e **(2)** usar o presente simples para o
que acontece **agora** (*"eu faço isso agora"*), quando o inglês quer o
continuous (*"I'm doing it now"*). Se um contraste ainda embola na sua cabeça,
**releia dois arquivos**: `lessons/2026-07-15-unit-003.md` (continuous vs simple,
o coração da confusão) e `lessons/2026-07-19-unit-007.md` (o *do/does* e o -s que
viaja). Esses dois cobrem as três decisões acima.

## 6. ✅ Gabarito — exercises

1. **run** — *"every quarter" = rotina → present simple, não a ação em andamento.*
2. **is shifting** — *"right now" = em progresso agora → present continuous.*
3. **Are** — *"confident" roda no* to be*; a pergunta só inverte* are*. Nunca* do
   *com* to be*.*
4. **Do** — *"approve" é verbo de ação → precisa do auxiliar* do*; verbos de ação
   não invertem.*
5. ✅ **"Why is the board delaying the AI budget?"** — *depois da palavra
   interrogativa, o* to be *ainda vem antes do sujeito.*
6. ✅ **"He doesn't approve early-stage bets."** — *o -s já está em* doesn't*; o
   verbo principal volta à base. Não marque a negativa duas vezes.*
7. ✅ **"The market is very competitive right now."** — *o inglês nunca omite o
   verbo* to be*; o português às vezes deixa implícito, o inglês não.*
8. **Does / review** — *"every quarter" = rotina → present simple question.* Is /
   reviewing *perguntaria só sobre este momento, não sobre a função habitual.*
9. ✅ **"We don't invest in unproven vendors."** — *o inglês exige o auxiliar*
   do/does*; não dá para negar um verbo puro com um* not *solto, como o "não" faz
   em português.*
10. **Are / rolling** — *"right now" = ação em andamento → present continuous; a
    pergunta inverte* are*, não usa* do*.*
