## 1. 📘 Unit 002 — am/is/are (questions and negatives)

---

## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Levi:** Are you on the incident response team?
> **Ana:** No, I'm not. I'm on the platform team. Is Marco available?
> **Levi:** He isn't here today. Is the deployment finished?
> **Ana:** Yes, it is. But the monitoring dashboard isn't ready yet.

Notice the pattern? *Are you...*, *I'm not*, *Is Marco...*, *He isn't*, *it is*, *isn't ready* — these are the building blocks of today's unit.

### The rule

**Questions** — move am/is/are to the front, before the subject:

| Statement | Question |
|---|---|
| You are available. | **Are** you available? |
| She is the team lead. | **Is** she the team lead? |
| They are ready. | **Are** they ready? |

**Short answers** — always match the verb:

- "Is the PR merged?" → "Yes, it **is**." / "No, it **isn't**."
- "Are you the on-call engineer?" → "Yes, I **am**." / "No, I**'m not**."
- ⚠️ Never contract a positive short answer: **"Yes, I am."** ✅ — **"Yes, I'm."** ❌

**Negatives** — add *not* immediately after am/is/are:

| Full form | Contraction |
|---|---|
| I am not | I'm not |
| He/She/It is not | He/She/It **isn't** |
| We/You/They are not | We/You/They **aren't** |

Contractions (isn't, aren't) are the normal choice in speech and informal writing. Use full forms only for emphasis: *"The system is **not** broken — it's just slow."*

## 3. ✏️ 8 Example Sentences

1. **Is the pull request ready for review?** — Yes, it is.
2. **The new API endpoint isn't** working in production yet.
3. **Are you** the on-call engineer this week?
4. **Our ML model isn't** accurate enough for this use case.
5. **Is the team** aligned on the technical approach?
6. **They aren't** available for the sprint planning meeting.
7. **Am I** correct that the deadline is Friday?
8. **She isn't** familiar with the new infrastructure yet.

---

## 4. ⚠️ 3 Common Mistakes

❌ **"He not is ready."** → ✅ **"He isn't ready."**
*Why: "not" always goes AFTER the verb (am/is/are), never before it.*

❌ **"Yes, I'm."** (short answer) → ✅ **"Yes, I am."**
*Why: English never contracts the last word of a positive short answer. The contraction "I'm" can't stand alone at the end.*

❌ **"Is they on the team?"** → ✅ **"Are they on the team?"**
*Why: "they" takes "are", not "is". Subject-verb agreement applies in questions too.*

## 6. 💡 Memory Tip

Think of a **light switch**:
- To make a **question**, flip the switch — move is/are to the front of the sentence.
- To make a **negative**, leave it in place and add "not" (or -n't) right after.
- Positive short answer? The switch stays **fully ON** — no contraction: "Yes, it **is**."

## 7. 🎤 Pronunciation Guide

### Key sounds

| Word | IPA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| is | /ɪz/ | Short /ɪ/ — not "eez" |
| isn't | /ˈɪznt/ | Ends in consonant cluster; no extra vowel ⚠️ |
| are | /ɑːr/ | Long open /ɑː/ — like "ah" ⚠️ |
| aren't | /ˈɑːrnt/ | Long /ɑː/ + consonant cluster ⚠️ |
| am | /æm/ | Short /æ/ — between "ah" and "eh" ⚠️ |

### Tips for Brazilian Portuguese speakers

- **"isn't"** — Brazilians often add a vowel at the end: *"izin-tchi"* → Aim for a clean /nt/ cluster with no final vowel.
- **"aren't"** — Portuguese doesn't have the /ɑː/ sound. Open your mouth wide, like a doctor asking you to say "ah", then add -rnt.
- **"are you"** in fast speech sounds like /ˈɑːrjə/ — *"Arya ready?"* — this is normal and natural.
- **"am I"** — the /æ/ is tense and front, not the open /a/ of Portuguese.

### Pronunciation Practice — say each phrase out loud

1. *Are you on the team?* — /ɑːr juː ɒn ðə tiːm/
2. *It isn't finished.* — /ɪt ˈɪznt ˈfɪnɪʃt/ ⚠️
3. *They aren't available.* — /ðeɪ ˈɑːrnt əˈveɪləbl/ ⚠️
4. *Is he the lead engineer?* — /ɪz hiː ðə liːd ˌendʒɪˈnɪər/
5. *No, I'm not.* — /nəʊ aɪm nɒt/
6. *Yes, it is.* — /jɛs ɪt ɪz/ ⚠️ (short, clipped — not "yeees, iiit iiiz")

## 10. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

Hoje estudamos como transformar frases com am/is/are em **perguntas e negativas**. Para fazer perguntas, o verbo vai ANTES do sujeito: *"Is she ready?"* — não *"She is ready?"*. Nas respostas curtas afirmativas, NUNCA usamos contração: *"Yes, I am."* ✅ — *"Yes, I'm."* ❌. Esse é um dos erros mais comuns de falantes de português.

Para as negativas, o "not" vem logo depois do verbo: *I'm not / He isn't / They aren't*. As contrações (isn't, aren't) são a forma natural na fala e na escrita informal. Evite colocar o "not" antes do verbo: ❌ *"He not is ready"* → ✅ *"He isn't ready"*. No trabalho, essas estruturas aparecem o tempo todo: perguntas sobre status, negações em code reviews, confirmações em reuniões.
