# Unit 006 — I don't... / Do you...? (present simple negative and questions)

The -s rule was the key challenge: third-person singular (he/she/it/the team) always adds **-s** to the verb in positive statements.

👉 **Answers:**

2. **doesn't use** — third person singular → doesn't + base verb (not "don't use").
3. **b)** — *reviews* → always add -s for he/she/it in positive statements.

## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Slack thread — #engineering-culture:**
>
> *Anya: "Hey Levi, do you usually join the sprint demos on Fridays?"*
>
> *Levi: "Not always — I don't attend every week because I often have stakeholder calls at the same time. But I do watch the recordings."*
>
> *Anya: "Does your team record every demo?"*
>
> *Levi: "Yes, they do. They post the link in #sprint-demos within an hour."*
>
> *Anya: "And do you give feedback asynchronously after?"*
>
> *Levi: "Sometimes. I don't comment unless I have something specific to add."*

Read again: **do you…? / I don't / does your team…? / Yes, they do.** These are the mechanics of today's unit — how to make negatives and questions in the present simple.

### The rule

To negate or question in the present simple, you need the **auxiliary do** or **does** — the main verb alone cannot do this job.

#### Negatives

| Subject | Form |
|---|---|
| I / you / we / they | **don't** + base verb |
| he / she / it | **doesn't** + base verb |

> ✅ *"I don't attend every demo."*
> ✅ *"The model doesn't support streaming yet."*
> ❌ *"She doesn't reviews PRs."* → The main verb drops the **-s** when **doesn't** is already carrying it.

#### Questions

| Subject | Form |
|---|---|
| I / you / we / they | **Do** + subject + base verb …? |
| he / she / it | **Does** + subject + base verb …? |

> ✅ *"Do you use feature flags?"*
> ✅ *"Does she attend the architecture review?"*
> ❌ *"Does she attends?"* → Never -s on the main verb when **does** is present.

#### Short answers

| Question | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Do you…? | Yes, I **do**. | No, I **don't**. |
| Does he/she/the team…? | Yes, he/she **does**. | No, he/she **doesn't**. |

#### Wh-questions

Add a question word **before** do/does — word order stays the same:

> *"Where do you work?"*
> *"What does your team use for incident management?"*
> *"How often does the model need fine-tuning?"*

## 3. ✏️ 8 Example Sentences

1. **"I don't push directly to main — we require pull requests for every change."** — negative, present simple, engineering practice
2. **"Does your CI pipeline run integration tests on every commit? Ours doesn't."** — question + short negative answer, DevOps
3. **"She doesn't own a specific product area — she works across the entire platform."** — negative, Staff engineering role description
4. **"Do you use TypeScript on the backend? Our team does, but only on newer services."** — question + short positive answer, software engineering
5. **"The new AI model doesn't hallucinate as much on structured prompts."** — negative, AI/ML observation
6. **"What does a Principal engineer actually do that a Staff engineer doesn't?"** — wh-question with embedded negative, tech leadership
7. **"I don't check email in the morning — it breaks my deep-work focus."** — negative, personal work habit
8. **"Does she live near the office, or does she always work remotely?"** — two questions in one, everyday context

## 5. 💡 Memory Tip

**The "seesaw" rule — when does goes up, the -s drops:**

The -s on the main verb belongs in **positive statements only** (she works, he reviews, it processes).

The moment **does / doesn't** enters the sentence, the main verb goes back to its bare base form:

| Positive | Negative | Question |
|---|---|---|
| She **works** | She **doesn't work** | **Does** she **work**? |
| He **reviews** | He **doesn't review** | **Does** he **review**? |

Think of it as a seesaw: **does** takes the -s so the main verb doesn't have to.

## 8. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

A **forma negativa** do presente simples usa **don't** (para I/you/we/they) ou **doesn't** (para he/she/it) antes do verbo principal, que sempre fica na **forma base** — sem o -s: *"She doesn't review"* (nunca *"doesn't reviews"*). As **perguntas** seguem a estrutura **do/does + sujeito + verbo base**: *"Do you work remotely?"*, *"Does she attend the standup?"*. Respostas curtas usam só o auxiliar: *"Yes, I do."*, *"No, she doesn't."* — nunca repita o verbo principal na resposta curta.

O erro mais frequente para brasileiros é manter o **-s no verbo principal** quando o auxiliar já indica a terceira pessoa: *"Does she works?"* está errado — o correto é *"Does she work?"*. Outra armadilha clássica é tentar negar sem o auxiliar: *"She reviews not PRs"* é inglês inválido; o correto é *"She doesn't review PRs"*. O auxiliar **do/does** é **obrigatório** em negativas e perguntas no presente simples — não é opcional como em português. Quando você dominar esse padrão, negativas e perguntas vão parecer automáticas.
