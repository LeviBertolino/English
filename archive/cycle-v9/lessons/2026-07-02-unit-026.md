# Unit 026 — Reported speech (He said that...)

## 0. 🔁 Warm-up: Quick Recall (≈60 seconds)

Yesterday's lesson — **Unit 025: have something done** — covered the causative structure: **have/get + object + past participle**, used when someone else does the action for you (*"We **had** the pipeline **migrated** by the platform team"*, *"I'm **getting** my laptop **repaired**"*). The memory tip was "HAVE = hand it off" — the object gets the "done" sticker, not the person doing it.

Three rapid recall questions:

1. Fix the error: *"We had the DBA **to review** it."*
2. Which is more informal — *"have"* or *"get"* in this structure?
3. True or false: *"We had our servers hacked last month"* means **we** arranged the hack.

👉 **Answers:**
1. ✅ *"We had it **reviewed** by the DBA."* — past participle, not infinitive
2. ✅ **"get"** is the more informal/casual choice
3. ✅ **False** — this use describes something unfortunate that happened *to* you, not something you arranged

## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Levi, catching up a teammate after standup:**
>
> **Teammate:** Hey, what did the tech lead say about the outage?
>
> **Levi:** She **said** the root cause **was** a bad config push, and that the team **was working** on a permanent fix.
>
> **Teammate:** Did she mention the postmortem?
>
> **Levi:** Yeah — she **said** she **would send** it by Friday, and that she **had already talked** to the on-call engineer.
>
> **Teammate:** Good. Did anyone ask about the client impact?
>
> **Levi:** I **told** her I **needed** the numbers before the exec sync.

Notice:
- The tech lead's actual words were probably: *"The root cause **is** a bad config push"* and *"I **will send** it by Friday."*
- When Levi reports what she said, the verb tense **shifts back one step into the past**: *is → was*, *will send → would send*, *have talked → had talked*.
- Pronouns and time words also shift to match the new speaker's perspective (*"I" → "she"*, *"today" → "that day"*, etc., though we keep it simple here).

### The rule

When you report what someone said (using **said that / told someone that**), the verb usually moves **one tense back** — this is called **backshift**:

| Direct speech | Reported speech |
|---|---|
| present simple → *"I **need** more time."* | past simple → She said she **needed** more time. |
| present continuous → *"We **are testing** it."* | past continuous → He said they **were testing** it. |
| present perfect → *"I **have finished**."* | past perfect → She said she **had finished**. |
| past simple → *"It **crashed**."* | past perfect → He said it **had crashed**. |
| will → *"I **will fix** it."* | would → She said she **would fix** it. |
| can → *"I **can help**."* | could → He said he **could help**. |

> ⚠️ **that is optional**: *"She said (that) she was busy."* Both are correct.
> ⚠️ Don't confuse **say** and **tell**: *say something* / *tell someone something*. ❌ *"She told that..."* → ✅ *"She said that..."* or *"She told me that..."*
> 💡 If the fact reported is **still true or general**, backshift is often skipped: *"She said the sun **rises** in the east."*

## 3. ✏️ 8 example sentences

1. The Staff engineer **said** the new architecture **would reduce** latency by half.
2. Our VP **told** the team **that** the roadmap **had changed** after the client call.
3. She **said** she **was reviewing** my PR and **would** approve it by end of day.
4. The AI team **reported** that the model **had passed** all evaluation benchmarks.
5. He **said** he **needed** more context before making a decision.
6. My friend **told** me she **was moving** to a new apartment next month.
7. They **said** the flight **had been** delayed by two hours.
8. She **mentioned** that she **couldn't** attend the meeting on Friday.

## 4. 🛠️ Practice — recognition + production

**Recognition/gap-fill:**
Convert to reported speech: Direct: *"I am fixing the bug right now."* → Reported: *"He said he ___ ___ the bug right then."*

✅ **Answer:** *"He said he **was fixing** the bug right then."*

**Production task:**
Write 2 sentences reporting something a colleague or manager recently told you (a status update, a decision, a deadline), using reported speech.

**Model answer:**
- *"She said the deployment had gone smoothly and that she would share the metrics tomorrow."*
- *"He told me the client had approved the proposal."*

---

## 5. 💡 Memory tip

**One step back in time.** Reported speech is like recalling a conversation the next day — everything that was "now" becomes "then." Present → past, will → would, can → could. If direct speech is a snapshot, reported speech is the memory of it, always one tense further back.

## 8. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

Hoje aprendemos o **discurso indireto (reported speech)**: quando repetimos o que alguém disse, o verbo geralmente "recua" um tempo verbal — presente vira passado, *will* vira *would*, *can* vira *could*, e o presente perfeito vira o passado perfeito (*had + particípio*). Por exemplo, *"I need more time"* se torna *"She said she needed more time"*.

Cuidado com dois pontos: (1) não confunda **say** e **tell** — usamos *tell someone that...*, mas nunca *"told that..."* sem a pessoa; (2) se o fato relatado ainda é verdade ou é uma verdade geral, não é obrigatório mudar o tempo verbal. Essa regra é muito útil no trabalho para relatar decisões, updates de status e conversas com o time.
