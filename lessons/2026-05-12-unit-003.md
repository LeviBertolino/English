## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Levi:** Hey Ana, where's Marco?
> **Ana:** He's in a meeting right now. He's presenting the Q2 roadmap.
> **Levi:** And the new engineer — what is she working on?
> **Ana:** She's setting up the local environment. The DevOps team is also deploying the new service today.
> **Levi:** Got it. I'm reviewing the architecture doc in the meantime.

Notice: *he's presenting*, *she's setting up*, *the team is deploying*, *I'm reviewing* — all describe actions **happening right now or around this time**.

### The rule

**Present continuous = am/is/are + verb + -ing**

| Subject | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I | am + -ing | I **am reviewing** the PR. |
| He/She/It | is + -ing | She **is deploying** the service. |
| We/You/They | are + -ing | They **are refactoring** the codebase. |

Use it when something is **happening right now** (this moment) or **around now** (this week, this sprint) — not necessarily at this exact second.

**Spelling rules for -ing:**
- Most verbs: just add -ing → *work → working*, *deploy → deploying*
- Verb ends in -e: drop the e → *write → writing*, *use → using*
- Short verb ending in one vowel + one consonant: double the consonant → *run → running*, *sit → sitting*, *get → getting*
- ⚠️ Verbs ending in -w, -x, -y don't double → *fix → fixing*, *show → showing*

## 3. ✏️ 8 Example Sentences

1. **I am reviewing** the pull request right now — give me five minutes.
2. **The AI team is training** a new model this sprint.
3. **We are migrating** the database to the cloud this quarter.
4. **She is leading** the architecture review session today.
5. **The deployment pipeline is failing** — someone needs to check the logs.
6. **They are building** a new feature for the enterprise clients.
7. **I'm working from home** this week because of the conference.
8. **The coffee machine is making** a strange noise again.

---

## 4. ⚠️ 3 Common Mistakes

❌ **"I am work on it."** → ✅ **"I am working on it."**
*Why: You must add -ing to the verb. am/is/are alone is not enough.*

❌ **"She is deploy the service."** → ✅ **"She is deploying the service."**
*Why: The verb always needs the -ing ending in present continuous.*

❌ **"I working on the PR."** → ✅ **"I am working on the PR."**
*Why: You can't drop am/is/are. Both parts of the structure are required.*

## 6. 💡 Memory Tip

Think of **-ing** as a "spinning wheel" — it shows the action is **in motion, right now**. You always need **am/is/are** to start the wheel spinning. No starter → no spin.

`am / is / are + [verb]-ing`

## 7. 🎤 Pronunciation Guide

### Key sounds

| Word/phrase | IPA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| working | /ˈwɜːrkɪŋ/ | -ing is /ɪŋ/, not /in/ or /iŋ/ ⚠️ |
| deploying | /dɪˈplɔɪɪŋ/ | 3 syllables; don't rush the -ing |
| reviewing | /rɪˈvjuːɪŋ/ | re-VIEW-ing — 3 syllables |
| I'm | /aɪm/ | Fast — one syllable, not "I am" in casual speech |
| she's | /ʃiːz/ | Ends in /z/, not /s/ ⚠️ |
| building | /ˈbɪldɪŋ/ | Short /ɪ/ — not /buːldɪŋ/ ⚠️ |

### Tips for Brazilian Portuguese speakers

- **-ing ending**: Brazilians often say *"workin'"* (dropping the /ŋ/) or *"working-ee"*. The correct ending is /ɪŋ/ — short /ɪ/ followed by a nasal /ŋ/. Press the back of your tongue to the soft palate for the final sound.
- **"she's working"**: the contraction *she's* sounds like /ʃiːz/. The -s is voiced (/z/), not /s/.
- **"I'm reviewing"**: in natural speech this runs together — *"aym rih-VYOO-ing"*. No pause between "I'm" and "reviewing."
- **"building"** ⚠️: the vowel in "build" is short /ɪ/. Brazilians may say /buːldɪŋ/ — keep it short.

## 10. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

O **presente contínuo** (present continuous) descreve ações que estão acontecendo agora ou em torno do momento presente — como esta semana ou este sprint. A estrutura é: **am/is/are + verbo + -ing**. Exemplos: *"I am reviewing the PR"*, *"The team is deploying the service"*, *"We are building a new feature."*

Dois erros muito comuns: esquecer o -ing (*"I am work"* ❌) ou esquecer o am/is/are (*"I working"* ❌). Ambas as partes são obrigatórias. Sobre a pronúncia, preste atenção ao final -ing: o som correto é /ɪŋ/, não /in/ nem /iŋ/. E as contrações (*I'm*, *she's*, *they're*) são o padrão na fala — use-as sem medo no dia a dia.
