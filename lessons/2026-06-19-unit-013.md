## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Engineering retrospective — Monday morning:**
>
> **Manager:** What were you working on when the staging environment went down on Friday?
>
> **Levi:** I was writing the integration tests for the new auth service. The team was deploying a config change at the same time — we didn't coordinate.
>
> **Manager:** Was the on-call engineer monitoring the alerts?
>
> **Levi:** She was — but she was also handling another incident. The alert fired at 4:47 PM while she was reviewing a hotfix.
>
> **Manager:** OK. So at 4:47, two things were happening simultaneously: the deployment and the alert. Got it.

Notice: **was/were + verb-ing** describes what was **in progress** at a moment in the past. It's the "background action" — still running when something else happened, or happening at the same time as something else.

### The rule

The **past continuous** describes an action that was **in progress** at a specific point in the past.

**Form:**

| Subject | was/were | verb + -ing |
|---|---|---|
| I / he / she / it | **was** | working / doing / reviewing |
| we / you / they | **were** | working / doing / reviewing |

| Structure | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Affirmative | Subject + was/were + verb-ing | *She was writing the runbook.* |
| Negative | Subject + wasn't/weren't + verb-ing | *They weren't monitoring the logs.* |
| Yes/No question | Was/Were + subject + verb-ing + ? | *Were you working on the migration?* |
| Wh- question | Wh- + was/were + subject + verb-ing + ? | *What was the team testing at 3pm?* |

**Two classic uses:**

1. **Action in progress at a past moment** — something was already happening when we "zoom in" on a specific time.
   > *At 2am, the on-call engineer was investigating a memory leak.*

2. **Interrupted action** — past continuous sets the background; simple past describes the interruption (often with **when**).
   > *I was reviewing the PR when the pipeline failed.*
   > *(background: reviewing → interrupted by: pipeline failed)*

   Or two parallel actions with **while**:
   > *While she was presenting the roadmap, he was pushing the hotfix.*

> **Key distinction:** Past continuous = action **in progress** (ongoing). Simple past = action **completed** (done).
> - *"I wrote the test."* → finished, done.
> - *"I was writing the test."* → in progress at some past moment (possibly interrupted, possibly completed later).

## 3. ✏️ 8 Example Sentences

1. **"The senior engineer was reviewing the pull request when Slack went down — she had to finish the review by email."** — engineering, interrupted action
2. **"At the time of the incident, three services were running health checks simultaneously, which overloaded the database."** — incident analysis, simultaneous actions
3. **"What were you deploying when the memory alert fired? That context will help us understand the spike."** — engineering leadership, Wh- question
4. **"The AI team was training the new model while the infrastructure team was migrating the storage backend — the resource contention was expected."** — tech leadership, parallel background actions
5. **"I wasn't attending the planning meeting because I was debugging a race condition in the async queue."** — engineering, negative + reason
6. **"Were you working from home when the power outage hit the office? The team couldn't reach the on-prem servers."** — work context, yes/no question
7. **"She was cooking dinner when her phone buzzed — a PagerDuty alert from the production cluster."** — everyday life interrupted by work
8. **"They weren't sleeping — they were watching the deploy dashboard from midnight until 2am, waiting for the metrics to stabilize."** — everyday life / on-call culture

## 5. 💡 Memory Tip

**Think of past continuous as a "movie frame."** The simple past is a photograph — a completed event. The past continuous is a freeze-frame from a movie: the action was **rolling** when you pressed pause.

> *"I was writing code"* → you pressed pause mid-keystroke.
> *"I wrote code"* → the scene is over; you see the finished result.

When you use **"when"** in a past story, ask: which action is the **running movie** (past continuous) and which is the **interruption** (simple past)? The interruption snaps you out of the movie.

## 8. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

O **past continuous** (passado contínuo) descreve uma ação que estava **em andamento** em um momento específico do passado. A forma é **was/were + verbo-ing**: *I was working*, *they were deploying*, *she wasn't monitoring*. Use *was* para I/he/she/it e *were* para we/you/they. Nas perguntas, inverta a ordem: *"Were you working?"* / *"What was the team testing?"*

Os dois usos mais comuns são: **(1) ação em progresso em um momento passado** — *"At 3am, the on-call engineer was investigating a leak"* — e **(2) ação interrompida**, onde o past continuous cria o fundo e o simple past descreve a interrupção: *"I was writing the spec when the CTO called"*. A palavra-chave é quase sempre **when** (para a interrupção) ou **while** (para duas ações paralelas). O ponto de confusão mais comum é misturar os dois tempos: lembre-se que o passado contínuo é o "filme rodando" e o simple past é o "evento que pausou o filme". A Unit 014 de amanhã vai direto nessa distinção — hoje é o momento perfeito para fixar a forma e o conceito básico.
