## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Levi:** Hey, are you joining the architecture review in 10 minutes?
> **Dev:** No, I'm not — I'm dealing with a production incident. Is the service still returning 500s?
> **Levi:** It was earlier. Are the on-call engineers looking at it?
> **Dev:** Yes, they are. What are you working on right now?
> **Levi:** I'm writing the post-mortem. Is anyone taking notes in the retro?
> **Dev:** I think so — is the PM recording it?

Notice: *Are you joining?*, *Is the service returning?*, *Are the engineers looking?*, *What are you working on?*, *Is anyone taking notes?*, *Is the PM recording?* — all are questions formed from present continuous.

### The rule

To make a **present continuous question**, move **am/is/are in front of the subject**:

| Statement | Question |
|---|---|
| You **are** reviewing it. | **Are** you reviewing it? |
| She **is** deploying the service. | **Is** she deploying the service? |
| They **are** building a feature. | **Are** they building a feature? |

For **Wh- questions**, add the question word at the front:

| Wh- question | Formula |
|---|---|
| *What are you working on?* | What + are + subject + -ing |
| *Why is the pipeline failing?* | Why + is + subject + -ing |
| *Who is leading the retro?* | Who + is + subject + -ing |

**Short answers** — affirmative answers can't be contracted:

| Question | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Are you reviewing? | Yes, I **am**. ✅ (NOT *Yes, I'm.*) | No, I'm not. |
| Is she deploying? | Yes, she **is**. | No, she isn't. |
| Are they building? | Yes, they **are**. | No, they aren't. |

## 3. ✏️ 8 Example Sentences

1. **Are you reviewing** the pull request right now? — "Yes, I am. Give me 10 minutes."
2. **Is the deployment pipeline failing** again? — "No, it isn't — it finished 5 minutes ago."
3. **What is the AI team working on** this sprint?
4. **Are they migrating** the auth service to the new cluster this quarter?
5. **Is she leading** the incident response call?
6. **Why is the build taking** so long? — "It's running the full integration suite."
7. **Are you coming** to the stand-up? — "No, I'm not — I'm in another call."
8. **What is he watching?** — "He's watching the match — the World Cup is on."

---

## 4. ⚠️ 3 Common Mistakes

❌ **"Are you work on it?"** → ✅ **"Are you working on it?"**
*Why: The -ing ending is still required in questions — the structure is am/is/are + subject + verb-ing.*

❌ **"Is they building the feature?"** → ✅ **"Are they building the feature?"**
*Why: "They" always takes "are", even in questions. Is goes with he/she/it only.*

❌ **"Yes, I'm."** → ✅ **"Yes, I am."**
*Why: Short affirmative answers never use contractions. "Yes, I'm." is ungrammatical in English.*

## 6. 💡 Memory Tip

Think of it as a **flip**: statements put am/is/are after the subject — questions **flip** them to the front.

> *She is reviewing.* → Flip → *Is she reviewing?*

For Wh- questions: **Question word first, then flip.**

> *What + [she is reviewing] → What is she reviewing?*

## 7. 🎤 Pronunciation Guide

### Key words and phrases

| Word/Phrase | IPA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Are you | /ɑːr juː/ | Casual speech: /ɑːrjə/ ("are-ya") ⚠️ |
| Is she | /ɪz ʃiː/ | Two separate sounds — /z/ then /ʃ/ |
| What are they | /wɒt ɑːr ðeɪ/ | "What're they" in fast speech ⚠️ |
| working on | /ˈwɜːrkɪŋ ɒn/ | Don't add a /h/ before "on" |
| shipping | /ˈʃɪpɪŋ/ | Short /ɪ/ — not /ʃiːpɪŋ/ ⚠️ |
| building | /ˈbɪldɪŋ/ | Short /ɪ/ — not /buːldɪŋ/ ⚠️ |

### Tips for Brazilian Portuguese speakers

- **"Are you"** in natural speech contracts to /ɑːrjə/ ("are-ya"). Brazilians often pause between "are" and "you" — practice flowing them together.
- **"Is she"**: the /z/ in "is" and the /ʃ/ in "she" are two different sounds right next to each other. Don't blend them into one.
- **"What are they"**: in fast speech this becomes "What're they" — /wɒtər ðeɪ/. The "are" almost disappears.
- **Short /ɪ/** in "shipping", "building", "fixing": Brazilians often produce these as long /iː/. Keep them short.
- **"Yes, I am"**: stress "am" — *"Yes, I AM."* Don't reduce it. This is the point where contractions are forbidden.

## 10. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

Para formar uma **pergunta no presente contínuo**, você inverte o sujeito e o am/is/are: *"She is reviewing"* vira *"Is she reviewing?"*. Para perguntas com palavra interrogativa (Wh-), coloque a palavra primeiro e depois faça o mesmo flip: *"What is she reviewing?"*. A estrutura é sempre: **Wh- + am/is/are + sujeito + verbo-ing**.

Um ponto muito importante: nas **respostas curtas afirmativas**, nunca use contração. *"Yes, I'm."* está errado — o correto é *"Yes, I am."* / *"Yes, she is."* / *"Yes, they are."* Nas negativas, a contração é normal: *"No, I'm not."* / *"No, she isn't."*. E lembre: *"they"* sempre usa *"are"*, mesmo em perguntas — nunca *"Is they..."*.
