## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Slack — #engineering channel, 10:47 AM:**
>
> **Maya:** Hey Levi, quick question — is the auth RFC ready to share with the team?
>
> **Levi:** Not yet! I **'m writing** the last section right now. Sam **is testing** the new login flow on staging and **is finding** some edge cases. Priya **is reviewing** the security requirements in parallel.
>
> **Maya:** And the CI pipeline?
>
> **Levi:** **It's running** the full test suite — should finish in about 20 minutes. The team **is working** really well together today.
>
> **Maya:** 👍 I **'m heading** into a call now. I'll check Notion after.

Notice the pattern: every main verb uses **is / am / are + verb-ing**. Nothing has finished — everything is **in progress right now**, as they type. That's the present continuous.

### The rule

**Present continuous = am / is / are + verb-ing**

| Subject | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I | am + -ing | I'm writing the RFC. |
| You | are + -ing | You're reviewing the PR. |
| He / She / It | is + -ing | She's testing the API. |
| We / They | are + -ing | They're deploying to prod. |

**Use it for actions happening RIGHT NOW — at this exact moment.**

> *"I'm writing a function."* → happening as you say it
> *"The build is failing."* → happening right now
> *"They're refactoring the service."* → in progress at this moment

### Spelling rules for -ing

| Rule | Base verb | + -ing |
|---|---|---|
| Most verbs: just add -ing | work, review, test | working, reviewing, testing |
| Verb ends in -e: drop -e, add -ing | write, compile, make | writing, compiling, making |
| Short verb (consonant-vowel-consonant): double the final consonant | run, sit, stop, ship | running, sitting, stopping, shipping |

Key examples: *run → running* (double n), *write → writing* (drop e), *work → working* (just add -ing).

## 4. ⚠️ 3 Common Mistakes

❌ **"I am work from home today."** → ✅ **"I am working from home today."**
*Why: Present continuous requires the -ing form. "am work" breaks the structure — the auxiliary (am) must pair with verb-ing. Always: am/is/are + verb-ing.*

❌ **"I working on the RFC right now."** → ✅ **"I am working on the RFC right now."** *(or "I'm working…")*
*Why: You cannot drop am/is/are from the present continuous. The auxiliary verb is not optional — it carries the tense.*

❌ **"She is writting the report."** → ✅ **"She is writing the report."**
*Why: "write" ends in -e, so you drop the -e before adding -ing: write → writing. Doubling the consonant applies to short consonant-vowel-consonant verbs like "sit → sitting", not -e verbs.*

## 7. 🎤 Pronunciation Guide

### Key words and IPA

| Word / Phrase | IPA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| I'm working | /aɪm ˈwɜːkɪŋ/ | "I am" contracts to "I'm" — one syllable ⚠️ |
| she's testing | /ʃiːz ˈtestɪŋ/ | "she is" → "she's" |
| they're running | /ðeər ˈrʌnɪŋ/ | /ð/ sound — voiced dental fricative ⚠️ |
| we're shipping | /wɪər ˈʃɪpɪŋ/ | "we're" → /wɪər/, not /wiː.ɑːr/ |
| -ing ending | /ɪŋ/ | Never /ɪŋgi/ — no vowel after /ŋ/ ⚠️ |
| writing | /ˈraɪtɪŋ/ | "write" → drop -e → "writing" — 2 syllables |
| running | /ˈrʌnɪŋ/ | "run" → double-n → "running" — 2 syllables |
| reviewing | /rɪˈvjuːɪŋ/ | 3 syllables: re-VIEW-ing ⚠️ |
| investigating | /ɪnˈvestɪɡeɪtɪŋ/ | 5 syllables: in-VES-ti-ga-ting |

### Tips for Brazilian Portuguese speakers

- **-ing ending** ⚠️: Brazilians often add a vowel after -ing: *"working-ee"* or *"running-ee."* The sound /ŋ/ is a nasal stop — it ends with complete silence after it. Keep it clean: /wɜːkɪŋ/ — nothing after the /ŋ/.
- **Contractions** ⚠️: *"I am working"* sounds formal and slow in conversation. Native speakers almost always say *"I'm working."* Practice making contractions feel natural and quick — "I'm" is /aɪm/, one short syllable.
- **"they're"** ⚠️: The /ð/ sound (voiced dental fricative — tongue between teeth, vibrating). Same challenge as /θ/ from Unit 018's "think," but voiced this time. Put your tongue lightly between your teeth and hum. Common mistake: saying */der/* instead of /ðeər/.
- **"reviewing"** ⚠️: 3 syllables — *re-VIEW-ing* (stress on 2nd). Brazilians often stress the first: *RE-view-ing*. Shift that stress to the middle.

## 10. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

O **presente contínuo** (present continuous) é formado com **am / is / are + verbo-ing** e é usado para ações que estão acontecendo **agora, neste exato momento**. Pense numa fotografia: ela congela uma ação no meio do caminho. *"I'm writing the RFC"* (estou escrevendo o RFC agora), *"The pipeline is running"* (o pipeline está rodando agora), *"We're deploying to production"* (estamos fazendo deploy agora). A estrutura é sempre: **sujeito + am/is/are + verbo com -ing**.

Atenção às regras de escrita do -ing: verbos terminados em **-e** perdem o -e (*write → writing*, *make → making*); verbos curtos com consoante-vogal-consoante dobram a consoante final (*run → running*, *ship → shipping*); a maioria dos verbos simplesmente recebe -ing (*work → working*, *test → testing*). Dois erros para evitar: (1) **não omita o am/is/are** — *"I working"* está errado; use *"I am working"*; (2) **não esqueça o -ing** — *"I am work"* está errado; use *"I am working."* Na pronúncia, treine as contrações (*I'm, she's, they're, we're*) — elas são indispensáveis no inglês natural — e cuidado para não adicionar uma vogal depois do -ing: *working* termina em /ŋ/, não em /ŋi/.
