## 3. ✍️ Integrative Writing Task (self-directed)

Write a short paragraph (5–7 sentences) about **a technical incident, sprint, or project you worked on recently**. Use at least **3 of this week's structures**:

- Past simple negative (*didn't + base verb*)
- Past continuous to set the scene (*was/were + -ing*)
- Past continuous interrupted (*was doing… when…*)
- Present simple for team habits (*-s on he/she/it*)
- Present simple negative/question (*doesn't / do you?*)
- Present continuous for current work (*am/is/are + -ing*)

**Model paragraph:**

*"Last quarter, our team was migrating the legacy auth service to a new identity provider when we discovered a critical dependency on a deprecated API. We didn't plan for that edge case in the original RFC — the spec didn't mention it anywhere. While the engineers were refactoring the core module, the product team changed the scope of the project entirely. Our team normally doesn't allow scope changes mid-sprint, but this one couldn't wait. Right now, we're re-evaluating the timeline and re-writing the ADR to reflect what actually happened."*

> Structures used: past continuous scene (*was migrating*), past continuous interrupted (*were refactoring… changed*), past simple negatives (*didn't plan / didn't mention*), present simple habit (*doesn't allow*), present continuous current (*are re-evaluating, re-writing*).

## 4. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

**O que deve estar sólido agora:**

As seis unidades desta semana cobrem o núcleo de dois sistemas temporais essenciais do inglês. No **passado**: o auxiliar **did/didn't** transforma qualquer verbo (regular ou irregular) em negativa ou pergunta — e o verbo principal sempre fica na forma base (*"She didn't see"*, nunca *"didn't saw"*). O **past continuous** (*was/were + -ing*) define o pano de fundo de uma cena, enquanto o **past simple** descreve os eventos que aconteceram: *"I was reviewing the RFC when the alert fired."* No **presente**: o **present simple** cobre hábitos, rotinas e verdades permanentes, com **-s obrigatório na terceira pessoa do singular** (*"She reviews PRs every day"*, *"The system processes requests"*). Negativas e perguntas no presente simples exigem **do/does** como auxiliar, e o verbo principal perde o -s: *"Does she review?"*, não *"Does she reviews?"*. O **present continuous** (*am/is/are + -ing*) sinaliza atividade ativa: algo acontecendo agora, algo temporário, ou uma tendência em progresso.

**O que provavelmente ainda precisa de atenção:**

Dois padrões de erro aparecem com frequência nesta fase — vale monitorar nos próximos dias. (1) Usar o verbo no passado depois de *didn't*: *"didn't went"* → correto é *"didn't go"*; *"didn't reviewed"* → correto é *"didn't review"*. O *did/didn't* já carrega o passado — o verbo principal fica nu. (2) Manter o -s no verbo principal quando *does/doesn't* está presente: *"Does she works?"* → correto é *"Does she work?"* O *does* já carrega o -s; o verbo perde o dele. Se esses dois reflexos ainda causam hesitação, releia a seção 2 de **Unit 013** e a seção 2 de **Unit 006** rapidamente antes da semana que começa.
