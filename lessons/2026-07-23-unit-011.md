### 2. 🧠 Mini-context

> **CEO:** How did the AI steering committee land yesterday?
> **You:** It **went** well. We **presented** the 2027 roadmap, the board
> **asked** two hard questions, and the CFO finally **approved** the pilot budget.
> **CEO:** And the vendor call?
> **You:** I **spoke** to them at nine, we **agreed** on the scope, and they
> **sent** the revised contract an hour later.

Notice the shape before any rule: yesterday's Unit 010 (*was/were*) told you **how
things were** — states. This unit tells you **what people did** — actions:
*went, presented, asked, approved, spoke, agreed, sent*. All finished, all in the
past. Some just add **-ed** (*presented, asked, approved, agreed*); some change
their whole shape (*go → went, speak → spoke, send → sent*).

---

### 3. 📐 A regra (form)

The **past simple** is the verb form for a **finished action** in the past. It has
**one form for every subject** — no *he/she* -s to worry about.

| Type | Present | **Past** | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regular | work | **worked** | add **-ed** |
| Regular (ends in -e) | agree | **agreed** | add **-d** only |
| Regular (consonant + y) | study | **studied** | y → **ied** |
| Regular (short vowel+consonant) | stop | **stopped** | double the consonant + **-ed** |
| Irregular | go | **went** | new word — memorise |
| Irregular | speak | **spoke** | new word — memorise |
| Irregular | send | **sent** | new word — memorise |

**Key facts:**
- **Same form for all subjects:** *I worked, she worked, they worked* — no *-s*.
- **Regular verbs** end in **-ed** (pronounced /t/, /d/, or /ɪd/ — *asked* /t/,
  *agreed* /d/, *presented* /ɪd/). The spelling is what matters here.
- **Irregular verbs** don't follow the -ed rule; you learn each one. The most
  common business verbs are irregular: *go→went, make→made, take→took, get→got,
  say→said, tell→told, meet→met, buy→bought, bring→brought, come→came, give→gave,
  send→sent, speak→spoke, think→thought, win→won, spend→spent.*
- This unit is **positive statements only**. Negatives and questions (*didn't /
  Did you…?*) are the next unit.

---

### 4. 🎯 Quando usar — e quando não (THE core section)

**Why does this structure exist?** To report a **completed action at a finished
past time** — something that happened and is over. *We **launched** the pilot in
March. The board **approved** the budget yesterday.* It is the workhorse of every
recap, post-mortem, and status update about what already happened.

**Decision rule #1 — past simple (action) vs *was/were* (state).** This is the
pair from yesterday, now complete. *Was/were* says **how something was**; the past
simple says **what someone did**. Never fuse them:
- *The meeting **was** tense.* → a state (*be*). ✅
- *The board **rejected** the first draft.* → an action (past simple). ✅
- ❌ *The board **was rejected** the first draft* — mixing a state verb with an
  action. It's either *was tense* **or** *rejected*, never *was* + a past action
  verb.

**Decision rule #2 — past simple vs present perfect (the rival ahead).** Use the
**past simple** when the time is **finished and often stated**: *yesterday, last
quarter, in 2024, an hour ago, at nine.* If a specific finished past time is in
(or implied by) the sentence, it's past simple — not *have done*:
- *We **approved** the budget **yesterday**.* → finished time → past simple. ✅
- ❌ *We **have approved** the budget yesterday.* — *have done* can't take a
  finished-time marker like *yesterday*. (Full contrast comes in a later unit; the
  safe rule now: **stated past time → past simple**.)

**PT→EN mapping.** The past simple maps cleanly onto the Portuguese **pretérito
perfeito**: *"Apresentamos o roadmap"* → *"We **presented** the roadmap"*;
*"O CFO aprovou"* → *"The CFO **approved**"*; *"Eu falei com eles"* → *"I
**spoke** to them"*. Where it betrays Levi:
- **Irregular verbs have no rule** — *"eu fui" ≠ "I goed"*, it's *"I **went**"*.
  Portuguese irregulars don't line up with English ones, so each must be learned.
- **No *-ed* after *did*** (next unit, but the reflex starts now): the past lives
  in the main verb here — *"I **spoke**"*, not *"I did spoke"*.

**Top Brazilian-speaker errors:**
- ❌ *We **goed** to the board with three options.* → ✅ *We **went** to the board
  with three options.* — *"go" is irregular: go → **went**, never "goed".*
- ❌ *The team **spendt** two months on the migration.* → ✅ *The team **spent**
  two months on the migration.* — *"spend" is irregular → **spent**; don't add
  -ed to an irregular verb.*
- ❌ *Last year we **hire** two AI leads.* → ✅ *Last year we **hired** two AI
  leads.* — *a finished past time ("last year") needs the past form; the bare
  present "hire" leaves the sentence in the wrong tense.*

### 5. ✏️ 8 annotated examples

1. The board **approved** the AI pilot budget yesterday. — *regular verb + finished
   time ("yesterday") → past simple, not "has approved".*
2. We **presented** the 2027 vision to the C-level last Tuesday. — *regular -ed;
   an action we did, so past simple, not "was presented".*
3. I **spoke** to the vendor at nine and we **agreed** on the scope. — *"speak" is
   irregular (spoke); "agree" is regular (+d) — one sentence, both patterns.*
4. The transformation **went** slower than planned, so we **revised** the timeline.
   — *"go→went" (irregular) plus regular "revised"; two finished actions in
   sequence.*
5. Last quarter the data team **spent** most of its capacity on the migration. —
   *"spend→spent" irregular; "last quarter" fixes a finished past time.*
6. The CFO **cut** the innovation budget by ten percent in Q1. — *"cut" is
   irregular and doesn't change form (cut→cut); the time frame makes it past.*
7. We **studied** three markets before we **chose** the entry point. — *"study →
   studied" (y→ied); "choose→chose" irregular; a completed decision process.*
8. The kids **watched** a film and then **fell** asleep on the sofa. — *everyday
   life; regular "watched" + irregular "fall→fell".*

### 8. 🗣️ Para levar para a aula

- Ask your tutor to run a **"what happened last week" recap**: you narrate three
  real past actions from your week — *"I presented…, the board approved…, we
  spoke to…"* — using at least four **irregular** verbs (went, spoke, made, took,

- Then a **state-vs-action switch drill**: your tutor names a past moment (*"the
  kickoff", "the vendor call", "Q1"*) and you produce **one *was/were* sentence**
  (how it was) **and one past-simple action sentence** (what you did) for each —
  *"The kickoff **was** tense; the CEO **changed** the agenda."* This locks in the
  boundary between Unit 010 and Unit 011.

**Warm-up (Unit 010)**
1. **were** — *"results" is plural → were (was is only for singular subjects).*
2. **There were** — *"three open risks" is plural, so the past existence is
   "there were", never "there was three".*
3. ❌ *"had 48"* → ✅ **"Our CFO was 48 when she joined the board."** — *age uses
   "be" in the past too: was 48, never "had 48 years" (direct from "tinha 48
   anos").*

**Spaced review**
- **(Unit 004)** ✅ **"Why is the board delaying the AI budget?"** — *after the
  wh-word, the verb "is" still comes before the subject; you can't keep statement
  order ("Why the board is…").*

**Recognition drill**
1. ❌ *"goed"* → ✅ **"We went to market a month earlier than the competitor."** —
   *"go" is irregular: go → went, never "goed".*
2. ❌ *"stoped"* → ✅ **"The pilot stopped after the budget freeze."** — *"stop"
   ends in short vowel + single consonant → double the p: stopped.*
3. ❌ *"reject"* → ✅ **"Last year the board rejected the first version of the
   roadmap."** — *"last year" is a finished past time → past form "rejected", not
   the bare present.*
4. **"The kickoff was tense, so the CEO changed the agenda on the spot."** — *"was"
   fills the state slot (how the kickoff was); "changed" fills the action slot
   (what the CEO did). One word can't do both — "be" describes a condition, it
   doesn't perform the action.*

### 10. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

**A regra e quando usar.** O *past simple* é a forma dos verbos de **ação
terminada** no passado — o que alguém **fez** (não como algo **estava**, que é o
*was/were* de ontem). Tem **uma só forma para todos os sujeitos** (sem *-s* no
he/she). Verbos **regulares** ganham **-ed**, com pequenos ajustes de ortografia
(*agree→agreed*, *study→studied*, *stop→stopped*). Verbos **irregulares** não
seguem regra — são palavras novas a decorar, e justamente os mais usados no
trabalho são irregulares: *go→went, make→made, take→took, get→got, send→sent,
spend→spent, speak→spoke, say→said*. Use sempre que houver um **tempo passado
definido** (ontem, no trimestre passado, em 2024, às nove).

**As armadilhas.** (1) Não crie *-ed* em verbo irregular: é *"I **went**"* e
*"we **spent**"*, jamais *"goed"* ou *"spendt"*. (2) Não deixe a frase no
presente quando há tempo passado: *"Last year we **hired**"*, não *"we hire"*.
(3) Estado vs ação: *was/were* diz **como estava**, o past simple diz **o que se
fez** — nunca junte os dois (*"was rejected"* como ação está errado; ou *"was
tense"* estado, ou *"rejected"* ação). (4) Antecipando: com tempo passado
definido, use o past simple, não o *have done* — *"We approved it yesterday"*,
não *"We have approved it yesterday"*. Perguntas e negativas (*Did you…? / didn't*)
ficam na próxima unidade.
