# Unit 035 — Should (2)

## 0. 🔁 Warm-up: Quick Recall (≈60 seconds)

Yesterday was **Unit 034 — Should (1)**, where Levi told a teammate reviewing a PR: *"I think you **should** extract that validation logic into its own function,"* and *"You **shouldn't** merge without at least one test on a change like this."*

Three rapid recall questions:

1. Does **should** express a hard rule, or an opinion/recommendation?
2. Which is stronger: *"You should write tests"* or *"You have to write tests"*?
3. What's the more formal near-synonym of **should** we saw yesterday?

👉 **Answers:**
1. ✅ An **opinion/recommendation** — it's advice, not an obligation like must/have to.
2. ✅ *"You **have to** write tests"* — have to/must express requirement; should is just advice.
3. ✅ **ought to** — same meaning as should, but more formal and less common in everyday speech.

## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Levi, in an incident postmortem:**
>
> **Levi:** Looking back, we **should have added** a canary deployment before this rollout — it would have caught the regression in minutes instead of hours.
> **Teammate:** Yeah, in hindsight we **shouldn't have skipped** the load test either.
> **Levi:** Exactly. We **should have known** better after last quarter's outage.
> **Teammate:** Fair. I'll add both to the action items so we don't repeat this.

Notice:
- **should have + past participle** looks back at something that **didn't happen** but would have been the wise/advisable thing to do. It carries a sense of **regret or criticism** about the past.
- **shouldn't have + past participle** looks back at something that **did happen** but wasn't a good idea.
- This is different from yesterday's **should** (Unit 034), which gives advice about the **present/future**. Today's form judges a **past** action or decision.

### The rule

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **should have** + past participle | something didn't happen, but it would have been wise | We **should have tested** this on staging first. |
| **shouldn't have** + past participle | something happened, but it wasn't a good idea | You **shouldn't have deployed** on a Friday afternoon. |
| **should** + base verb (Unit 034, recap) | advice about now/future | You **should test** this on staging next time. |

> ⚠️ **should have** is often pronounced/written **"should've"** in speech — but never write **"should of"**. It's a common spelling mistake even for native speakers; the "of" is just how "have" sounds when reduced.
> ⚠️ Portuguese *"deveria ter feito"* (mas não fez) maps to **should have done**; *"não deveria ter feito"* (mas fez) maps to **shouldn't have done**. Don't confuse this backward-looking regret with the forward-looking advice of plain **should**.

## 3. ✏️ 8 example sentences

1. We **should have written** more unit tests before the release — the regression slipped straight through.
2. You **shouldn't have force-pushed** to the shared branch without warning the team.
3. I **should have caught** that null-pointer bug in code review.
4. The team **should have flagged** the scaling risk earlier in the design doc.
5. She **should have backed up** her laptop before the OS update — she lost two days of work.
6. You **shouldn't have eaten** so late; no wonder you slept badly.
7. We **should have left** the house earlier — now we're stuck in traffic.
8. He **shouldn't have shown up** without calling first.

✅ **Answer:** *"we **should have tested** it on staging first."*

## 5. 💡 Memory tip

**SHOULD HAVE = "shoulda, woulda, coulda."** It's the sound of hindsight — picture yourself looking back at a decision and sighing "I shoulda tested that." If you hear yourself saying "should of" in your head, that's your cue it's actually **should have**.

## 8. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

Hoje vimos **should have + particípio passado**, usado para julgar uma ação do **passado** que **não aconteceu** mas teria sido a atitude sensata (*"deveria ter feito"*), e **shouldn't have + particípio**, para uma ação que **aconteceu** mas não foi uma boa ideia (*"não deveria ter feito"*). É diferente do **should** da Unit 034, que dá conselho sobre o presente/futuro — hoje o foco é olhar para trás com arrependimento ou crítica.

O cuidado principal é de ortografia: em inglês falado, "should have" soa como "should've", o que leva muita gente (inclusive nativos) a escrever erroneamente **"should of"** — isso está sempre errado. Lembre-se também de não confundir esse uso retrospectivo com o **should** comum, que fala do que fazer agora, não do que já passou.
