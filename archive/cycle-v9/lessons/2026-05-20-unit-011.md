## 1. 📘 Unit 011 — I didn't... Did you...? (Past Simple — Negatives & Questions)

---

## 2. 🧠 Adquirir — Mini-context first, rule second

> **Levi:** Did you review the architecture proposal before the meeting?
> **Ana:** I started, but I didn't finish it. Did the team decide on the approach?
> **Levi:** We didn't reach a consensus — the discussion ran long. Did anyone push back on the event-sourcing idea?
> **Ana:** Yeah, Marcus did. He didn't think it was the right fit for our scale.
> **Levi:** Fair. I didn't expect that level of resistance, honestly.

Notice the pattern: *did you review*, *I didn't finish*, *did the team decide*, *we didn't reach*, *did anyone push back*, *he didn't think*, *I didn't expect*. These are **past simple negatives and questions** — the flip side of the affirmative forms from Unit 010.

### The rule

For past simple negatives and questions of all verbs **except "be"**, use **did/didn't** as the helper:

**Negative:**
> **Subject + didn't (did not) + base form of verb**
> *We didn't merge the PR.* / *She didn't attend the demo.*

**Question:**
> **Did + subject + base form of verb?**
> *Did you push the hotfix?* / *Did the tests pass?*

**Short answers:**
> *Yes, I/he/she/we/they did.* / *No, I/he/she/we/they didn't.*

**Critical rule — don't mix "did" with "be":**
> ❌ *Did he was on call?* → ✅ *Was he on call?*
> ❌ *He didn't be available.* → ✅ *He wasn't available.*
> "Be" has its own past question/negative forms: *was/were* / *wasn't/weren't*. "Did" is only for other verbs.

**The base form rule:** After *didn't* and *Did*, the main verb always goes back to its **base (dictionary) form** — because "did" already carries the past tense.
> ❌ *I didn't went to standup.* → ✅ *I didn't go to standup.*
> ❌ *Did you reviewed the PR?* → ✅ *Did you review the PR?*

## 3. ✏️ 8 Example Sentences

1. **We didn't deploy on Friday** — the rollback script didn't trigger automatically in the staging environment.
2. **Did you review the security findings before closing the ticket?** The compliance team didn't approve it yet.
3. **The CI pipeline didn't fail** on the first run — it broke after we added the integration test suite.
4. **Did the team decide on a migration strategy?** I didn't catch the end of the design review.
5. **She didn't respond to the RFC comments** — I think the deadline didn't reach her calendar.
6. **Did the sprint demo go well?** I didn't attend; I was in a 1:1 with my skip-level.
7. **I didn't sleep well last night** — the on-call alert went off at 3am.
8. **Did you eat before the all-hands?** I didn't — I was prepping the slides until the last minute.

---

## 4. ⚠️ 3 Common Mistakes

❌ **"I didn't went to the standup"** → ✅ **"I didn't go to the standup"**
*Why: After "didn't", the verb must be the base form. "Didn't" already signals the past — don't double-mark it.*

❌ **"Did you reviewed the PR?"** → ✅ **"Did you review the PR?"**
*Why: Same principle — "Did" carries the past. The main verb returns to base form. This catches even advanced learners off guard.*

❌ **"He didn't was available during the incident"** → ✅ **"He wasn't available during the incident"**
*Why: "Didn't" is only for verbs other than "be". For past "be", use "wasn't/weren't". Mixing them (*didn't was*, *didn't were*) is never correct.*

## 6. 💡 Memory Tip

Think of **"did"** as a **past-tense placeholder**: it absorbs all the "past-ness" so the main verb can relax back to its base form.

> *"Did" = already past → verb = base form (no "-ed", no irregulars)*

A useful mental check: if you wrote *"-ed"* or an irregular past after *didn't* / *did*, delete it.
- *didn't worked* → *didn't work* ✓
- *did he went* → *did he go* ✓

And the companion rule: **"did" ≠ "be"**. When you see *was/were*, negatives and questions don't use "did" — they flip the verb directly (*was → wasn't*, *were → weren't*).

## 7. 🎤 Pronunciation Guide

### Key words and phrases

| Word/Phrase | IPA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| didn't | /ˈdɪdənt/ | Two syllables: *DID-nt*; the "t" is often a glottal stop ⚠️ |
| did you | /dɪdʒu/ | Natural assimilation: "didja" in fast speech ⚠️ |
| didn't go | /ˈdɪdənt ɡəʊ/ | No extra vowel after the "t" ⚠️ |
| did the team | /dɪd ðə tiːm/ | "did" + weak "the" = fast /dɪdðə/ ⚠️ |
| we didn't | /wi ˈdɪdənt/ | Stress on *didn't*, not *we* |
| did it pass? | /dɪd ɪt pɑːs/ | |

### Tips for Brazilian Portuguese speakers

- **"didn't" final cluster /dnt/**: Brazilians frequently add a vowel after the final *t* — saying *"DID-en-tchi"*. Close the *n*, produce a light *t* (or glottal stop), and stop. No extra sound. ⚠️
- **"did you" → /dɪdʒu/ assimilation**: When "did" is followed by "you", the /d/ + /j/ naturally merge into /dʒ/ (like "j" in "jump"). This is standard — *"Didja review it?"* sounds natural; fighting it sounds stiff. ⚠️
- **The /ɪ/ in "did"**: This is a short, relaxed vowel — not the long /iː/ of "deed". Brazilians often over-lengthen it (saying "deed" instead of "did"). Keep it short and loose. ⚠️
- **Sentence stress in questions**: *"Did YOU review it?"* vs. *"Did you REVIEW it?"* — stress shifts based on what's new information. In most neutral questions, the content verb gets the stress: *"Did you re-VIEW it?"*

## 10. 🇧🇷 Resumo em português

O **passado simples negativo e interrogativo** de verbos regulares e irregulares (exceto "be") usa o auxiliar **"did"**: negativo → *didn't + infinitivo*; pergunta → *Did + sujeito + infinitivo?* A regra mais importante: após *didn't* ou *Did*, o verbo principal volta à **forma base** (infinitivo sem "to"), porque o "did" já carrega o tempo passado. Erros como *"I didn't went"* ou *"Did you reviewed?"* são muito comuns — o gatilho mental é: "did já é passado, então o verbo relaxa."

Atenção ao **"be"**: ele tem suas próprias formas negativas e interrogativas (*wasn't/weren't*, *Was/Were*) — nunca use *"didn't be"* ou *"Did he was"*. Na pronúncia, fique atento a dois pontos: (1) *"didn't"* termina com /nt/ sem vogal extra — não adicione um "i" final; (2) *"did you"* vira naturalmente /dɪdʒu/ ("didja") na fala conectada — isso é correto e esperado em inglês fluente.
